Travel Support

A limited number of travel grants will be available to help young students and early-career researchers to attend the Hinode-14/IRIS-11 meeting. We have confirmed support from ESA for scientists affiliated with institutions in ESA member states, from the U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF), from the Scientific Committee on Solar-Terrestrial Physics (SCOSTEP) and from the National Solar Observatory through the DKIST Ambassador Program (see below).

To submit your application, download and fill out the Travel Support Application form and send it to hinode14meeting@gmail.com along with a Curriculum Vitae by August 27, 2021. Applicants are strongly encouraged to submit an abstract to the conference.

Travel support applications will be assessed once the final format of the meeting is confirmed. In case of a FULLY VIRTUAL meeting, the LOC will consider waiving the registration fee for young participants.

We encourage attendance from participants of the DKIST Ambassador Program. Additionally, any US students that are part of an existing DKIST Science Use Case (SUC) of the Critical Science Plan, who can explain how what they are presenting at the Hinode-14/IRIS-11 meeting is related to the SUC, and who describe what level-2 DKIST data they will produce, can also contact the program for potential support (Science Lead: Dr. Han Uitenbroek, huitenbroek (at) nso.edu).
